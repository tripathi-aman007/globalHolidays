Top 7 Indian Natioanl Parks

Jim Corbett National Park (Uttarakhand)

Established in 1936, Jim Corbett National Park is one of the oldest national parks in India. It is renowned for its population of Bengal tigers, along with other wildlife such as leopards, elephants, and deer. The park is located in the foothills of the Himalayas and offers opportunities for wildlife safaris and birdwatching.

Ranthambore National Park (Rajasthan)

Located in the Sawai Madhopur district of Rajasthan, Ranthambore National Park is famous for its population of Bengal tigers. It is also home to a variety of other wildlife, including leopards, sloth bears, and marsh crocodiles. The park is known for its ancient Ranthambore Fort and picturesque landscapes.

Kaziranga National Park (Assam)

Kaziranga National Park, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, is situated in the state of Assam. It is renowned for its population of Indian rhinoceroses, as well as tigers, elephants, and water buffaloes. The park is also a haven for birdwatchers, with numerous species of birds found here.

Bandhavgarh National Park (Madhya Pradesh)

It is known for having one of the highest densities of Bengal tigers in India. The park also boasts diverse flora and fauna, including leopards, deer, and various bird species.

Gir National Park (Gujarat)

It is also known as Sasan Gir, is renowned as the last refuge of the Asiatic lions. Located in Gujarat, it is the only place in the world where Asiatic lions are found in the wild. Apart from lions, the park is home to leopards, deer, and numerous bird species.

Periyar National Park (Kerala)

It is situated in the Western Ghats of Kerala, is known for its rich biodiversity and scenic beauty. The park is famous for its population of Indian elephants, which can be observed during boat safaris on Periyar Lake. It is also home to tigers, leopards, and various species of birds.

Tadoba Andhari Tiger Reserve (Maharashtra)

It is located in the Chandrapur district of Maharashtra. It is one of the largest and oldest national parks in Maharashtra and is known for its tiger population. The reserve also supports a variety of other wildlife, including leopards, sloth bears, and wild dogs.
